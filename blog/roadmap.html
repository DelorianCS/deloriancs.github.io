Cybersecurity Roadmap

From absolute zero to professional penetration tester.

≈ 670–700 hours total • Resources: HackTheBox, TryHackMe, YouTube
1) Fundamentals 2) Pentesting fundamentals 3) Intermediate pentesting 4) Job finding & tips

1 — Fundamentals

Total study time: ~70h

▶ A+ Course — Professor Messer — playlist (~20h)

Networking basics, hardware, OS fundamentals.

▶ TryHackMe Pre Security Fundamentals — path (~10h)

Option A for absolute beginners; high-level intro + hands-on.

▶ Network+ — Professor Messer — playlist (~20h)

Option B if you prefer deeper networking fundamentals.

▶ Security+ — Professor Messer — course playlist (~20h)

Covers core security concepts and vocabulary for junior roles.

First certification: CompTIA Security+

2 — Introduction to Pentesting

Total study time: ~100h

▶ TryHackMe: Jr Penetration Tester — path (~70h)

Covers recon, web basics, privilege escalation samplers.

▶ TryHackMe: Offensive Pentesting — path (~50h)

Hands-on labs aligned with common job tasks.

Second certification: eJPT

3 — Intermediate Pentesting

Total study time: ~500h

▶ HackTheBox Academy: Penetration Tester Job Role Path — path (~300h)

Deep modules + labs; build routine and notes.

▶ TJNull – OSCP-style list — spreadsheet

Pick HTB boxes matching your gaps; hours vary.

▶ IPPSec CPTS Prep labs — YouTube playlist

Great long-form lab walkthroughs; replicate, don’t just watch.

Third certification: CPTS or OSCP (budget & preference)

4 — Job finding

Have projects, write-ups, and tools to strengthen your CV.

After Security+, apply for SOC L1 if you need income — it adds relevant experience while you continue labs.

Tips

  • Use Obsidian / Notion / CherryTree for structured notes.
  • Build your own cheatsheets — you will reuse them constantly.
  • Learn Python to automate recon, parsing, and small exploits.
  • Track hours and revisit weak areas; don’t follow any plan blindly.
Grand total study time (approx): ~700h
